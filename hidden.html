2025.12.20

Why is the unity of knowledge and action so difficult? It's mainly because we lack deep understanding of the consequences that action brings. Everyone knows that drugs and smoking are harmful to health, yet few quit because of this knowledge—they haven't felt the benefits of quitting, or in other words, they haven't deeply experienced the consequences of not quitting.

All life has the ability to adapt to its environment; living in the moment is an instinct. Most people don't consider long-term consequences. Only when the bitter fruits draw near do they sense crisis, but by then it's too late—many things in life offer no chance for trial and error. Some things do allow many trials; when pain is experienced repeatedly, one gradually achieves unity of knowledge and action in that matter. But when the pain is forgotten, one may follow impulse again...
